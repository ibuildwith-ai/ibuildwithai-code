In this episode, I sit down with Dustin Lehr, co-founder of Katilyst, to talk about why domain expertise alone is no longer the baseline for knowledge workers. We explore how vibe coding is changing what it means to be a builder, why future enterprises will increasingly value product-building skills alongside domain knowledge, and how knowledge workers can use AI to turn their expertise into real products, prototypes, and experiments. Dustin shares insights from his background in software engineering and cybersecurity to explain how building, not just knowing, is becoming essential for staying relevant and competitive.

### Key Topics Covered

1. Why domain expertise alone is no longer the baseline for knowledge workers
2. How vibe coding enables domain experts to become product builders
3. The growing expectation for product-building skills in future enterprises
4. Turning expertise into real products, prototypes, and experiments using AI
5. What it means to stay relevant and competitive as a knowledge worker
